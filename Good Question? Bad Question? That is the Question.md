“I’m having trouble writing my essay. Can anyone help me out?” Some people may look at this question with a sense of pity for the question
asker and an eagerness to offer help while others may look in disdain. Are the people in the former group justified in their feelings?
Throughout my academic career, I have constantly been told by my professors that “There are no such thing as stupid questions.” But how
accurate is this statement really?

In Eric Steven Raymond’s essay “How to Ask Questions the Smart Way”, Raymond quite vocally states his belief that stupid questions definitely
exist. Raymond has a list of criteria one should follow in order to be able to ask smart questions so as not to be completely ridiculed by the
online community of coders on various platforms such as StackOverflow.

Perhaps what professors were trying to convey when stating that “There are no such thing as stupid questions” was the idea that there is
nothing wrong with asking for clarification on ideas that may seem trivial or being curious about concepts that may appear to be tangential to
the topic on hand. The fact that students would have a curiosity about the topic being discussed suggests that they have an interest in what is
being taught and this curiosity shows their engagement in the material.
